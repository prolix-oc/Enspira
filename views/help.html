Help & Documentation

Welcome to Enspira's Help page! This guide explains how to use template variables in your character, world info, and scenario settings. Simply insert these variables in your templates, and they will be automatically replaced with your data.

Quick Reference

Commonly used variables:

{{user}} {{char}} {{socials}} {{socials.twitter}} {{datetime}} {{current_game}} {{current_views}}

Template Variables

Character Card & World Information Variables

  • {{user}} Your name as recognized by the assistant – typically your streaming username
  • {{char}} The name of your AI assistant or character
  • {{socials}} A pre-formatted list of all your linked social media accounts
  • {{socials.xxx}} A specific social media account where xxx is one of:
    tiktok youtube twitch twitter kick
  • {{datetime}} The current date and time

Scenario Variables

  • {{current_game}} The game you are currently playing, as retrieved from Twitch
  • {{current_views}} The current viewer count of your Twitch channel
  • {{current_follows}} Your current follower count from Twitch
  • Also available: {{user}}, {{char}}, {{socials}}, {{socials.xxx}}, and {{datetime}}

How to Use Template Variables

When editing your character, world, or scenario, simply place these variables within your content. For example, to display your name, include {{user}} where appropriate.

Example Template:

Hello, my name is {{char}} and I'm {{user}}'s AI assistant!
You can follow {{user}} on Twitter at @{{socials.twitter}}

The current game being played is {{current_game}}
There are {{current_views}} viewers watching right now!

Tips for Effective Templates

  • Always use the exact variable names as shown above (including curly braces)
  • For social media handles, replace xxx in {{socials.xxx}} with a valid platform name
  • Combine static text with variables to craft personalized messages
  • Preview your changes to see how the data populates
  • Be aware that some variables (like {{current_game}}) will only display data when your stream is active

This guide is designed to help you create dynamic, data-driven character and world settings for your stream. If you have questions, check our GitHub repository or reach out to the community.
