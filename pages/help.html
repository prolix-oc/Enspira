Help & Tutorial

Welcome to the Enspira Help page! This guide will show you how to edit your character, world info, and scenario cards using our custom templating engine. Simply insert the variables in your template, and they will be automatically replaced with your live data.

Quick Reference

Examples of commonly used variables:

{{user}} {{char}} {{socials}} {{socials.twitter}} {{datetime}}

Template Variable Definitions

Character Card & World Information Variables

  • ›
    {{user}} Your name as recognized by the assistant – typically your streaming username.
  • ›
    {{char}} The name of your AI assistant or character.
  • ›
    {{socials}} A pre-formatted list of all your linked social media accounts (e.g., "Prolix_GG", "prolix_oc", "Prolix").
  • ›
    {{socials.xxx}} A specific social media account. Replace xxx with one of these valid values:
    tiktok youtube twitch twitter kick
  • ›
    {{datetime}} The current date and time when the page is rendered.

Scenario Card Variables

  • ›
    {{current_game}} The game you are currently playing, as pulled from Twitch.
  • ›
    {{current_views}} The current viewer count of your channel from Twitch.
  • ›
    {{current_follows}} Your current follower count as provided by Twitch.
  • ›
    Also available: {{socials}}, {{socials.xxx}}, {{char}}, {{user}}, and {{datetime}}

How to Build Your Cards

When editing your character or world card, simply place these variables within your template. For instance, if you want to show your name, include {{user}} where appropriate. Similarly, use {{char}} to display your assistant's name.

Example:

Hello, my name is {{char}} and I'm {{user}}'s AI assistant!
You can follow {{user}} on Twitter at @{{socials.twitter}}

The current game being played is {{current_game}}
There are {{current_views}} viewers watching right now!

Tips for Using the Templating Engine

  • ✓
    Always verify that you are using the exact variable names as defined above.
  • ✓
    For social media handles, replace xxx in {{socials.xxx}} with a valid platform (e.g., twitter).
  • ✓
    You can combine static text with these variables to craft a personalized message or card for your channel.
  • ✓
    Preview your changes by saving and reloading the page to see how the data populates.

This tutorial is designed to simplify the process of creating dynamic, data-driven character and world cards for your streaming setup. If you have further questions, our support team is always here to help.
